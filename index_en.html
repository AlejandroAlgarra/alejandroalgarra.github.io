Biography

Noted by critics as one of the leading young talents of the Spanish national scene, the pianist Alejandro Algarra studied in his hometown, Granada, concluding them with his piano professor Antonio Sánchez Lucena. He expanded his training with great pianists like Josep Colom, Albert Attenelle, David Kuyken, Sergei Yerokhin, William Fong, Ramon Coll or Frank Wibaut, among others. He obtained the titles of University Expert and Master in Instrumental Interpretation -Piano specialty- by the International University of Andalusia, training with teachers as important as Christopher Elton, Joaquín Achúcarro, Domenico Codispoti, Bruno Canino, Dimitri Alexeev or Pascal Rogé.

His increasing concert activity has led him to act, both alone and accompanied by several orchestras, in a large number of concert halls, always with great critical success. He is a regular guest of relevant piano festivals, of which stand out the "Rafael Orozco" International Piano Festival of Córdoba, the "Conciertos de la Pedrera" in Barcelona, the "Gustavo Díaz Jerez" Festival in Tenerife, the Spring Festival of La Maestranza in Seville, the cycle "Grandes Intérpretes" in Torrelodones (Madrid), or the Festival Noches en los Jardines de Falla in Granada, apart from being invited to play recitals in both the national and international piano competition scene.

He has been awarded in a large number of competitions, among which we can mention the "Ciudad de Albacete" Piano Competition, the International Piano Competition "Ria de Vigo", the "Amparo Fandos" Prize in Torrent, the International Piano Competition "Frechilla-Zuloaga", the Barcelona Piano Academy, or the International Piano Competition of Ibiza.

Julio García Casas: “Music is mankind's heritage, and it must be cultivated”

Opera World interviewed Don Julio García Casas after the recital offered by the Grenadian pianist Alejandro Algarra in Seville the 15th of June, with Chopin's 24 Preludes as main course. With that interpretation, Algarra wanted to entertain this great energizer of Seville's cultural life. Born in Fregenal de la Sierra (Badajoz), but settled for decades in the Sevillain capital, Julio García Casas has presided over Juventudes Musicales of Seville for an incredibly long time, lending from this institution a fundamental support at the beginning of the careers of suceeding generations of Spanish pianists, from Rafael Orozco to Juan Pérez Floristán.
