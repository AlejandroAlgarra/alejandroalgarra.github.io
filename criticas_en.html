DELIGHTFUL BEETHOVEN

"It is difficult to play this concerto [Piano Concerto No. 5]. But this great young pianist, grandiose we might say, Alejandro Algarra, accepted a difficult challenge and brought it to a successful completion, with a performance worthy of all praise. From the brilliant beginnings to a majestic finale. Strength, passion, enthusiasm, beauty conveyed in a fortunate interpretation of this work which has seen so many performers shipwrecked."

-José Antonio Lacárcel, 2013

RECOMMENDED DISC OF THE MONTH

"The pianist Alejandro Algarra offers in his recording debut a double CD in which he shows two immense corpus of piano music: the preludes of Chopin and Rachmaninov. A major challenge, no doubt, and with a great outcome. The Preludes are interpreted in a reading full of sensitivity and elegance, with temperament, but also with finesse when necessary. Algarra is delicate, knowing how to enter into this microcosm that this CD represents, so imbued with sensations, colours and poetry. Entering each prelude with a different atmosphere, with musicality and a poetic touch that is always so necessary. [...] Alejandro Algarra has a mechanism that allows him to accomplish what he intends, and he brings a virtuosity that he knows how to put at the service of the musicality and expressiveness always prevailing. A piano party that will make you have a great time, with an impeccable sound shot."

-Revista Segarra, August 2022

Julio García Casas: “Music is mankind's heritage and it must be cultivated”

Opera World interviewed Don Julio García Casas after the recital offered by the Grenadian pianist Alejandro Algarra in Seville the 15th of June, with Chopin's 24 Preludes as main course. With that interpretation, Algarra wanted to entertain this great energizer of Seville's cultural life. Born in Fregenal de la Sierra (Badajoz), but settled for decades in the Sevillain capital, Julio García Casas has presided over Juventudes Musicales of Seville for an incredibly long time, lending from this institution a fundamental support at the beginning of the careers of suceeding generations of Spanish pianists, from Rafael Orozco to Juan Pérez Floristán.
